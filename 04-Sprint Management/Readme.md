
# Sprint Management

## Overview

The Sprint Management framework defines how delivery teams plan, execute, monitor, and review sprint commitments within the Delivery Command Center.

It provides governance, visibility, and control mechanisms to ensure sprint objectives are achieved while maintaining alignment with business priorities and delivery commitments.

---

## Objectives

- Improve sprint planning and commitment reliability.
- Increase delivery predictability and transparency.
- Enable proactive management of risks, blockers, and dependencies.
- Support effective sprint execution and stakeholder visibility.
- Drive continuous improvement through regular inspection and adaptation.

## Expected Outcomes

- Predictable and transparent sprint delivery.
- Improved commitment accuracy and capacity utilization.
- Early identification and management of delivery risks.
- Increased stakeholder confidence through delivery visibility.
- Continuous improvement of team performance and delivery effectiveness.

---

## Key Insights

- Sprint success begins with realistic planning and commitment.
- Delivery transparency enables proactive decision-making.
- Risks and blockers should be identified and addressed early.
- Continuous improvement is essential for sustainable delivery performance.
- Governance should enable delivery, not create unnecessary overhead.

---

## Governance Principles

- Commit based on available capacity and priorities.
- Maintain visibility throughout sprint execution.
- Escalate blockers and risks proactively.
- Focus on delivery outcomes and business value.
- Use retrospectives to drive continuous improvement.
