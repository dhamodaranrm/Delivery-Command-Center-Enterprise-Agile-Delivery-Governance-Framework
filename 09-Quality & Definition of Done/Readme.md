
# Quality & Definition of Done

## Overview

The Quality & Definition of Done framework establishes the standards, practices, and governance required to ensure consistent delivery of high-quality products. It defines quality expectations, completion criteria, review processes, and continuous quality improvement across the Agile delivery lifecycle.

---

## Objectives

- Establish consistent quality standards across delivery teams.
- Define clear completion criteria for backlog items and releases.
- Promote quality through continuous verification and validation.
- Reduce defects and improve delivery reliability.
- Foster a culture of continuous quality improvement.

## Expected Outcomes

- Consistent delivery of high-quality products.
- Clear Definition of Done across Agile teams.
- Reduced defects and improved product reliability.
- Increased delivery confidence through standardized quality practices.
- Continuous improvement supported by measurable quality metrics.

---

## Key Insights

- Quality is a shared responsibility across the delivery team.
- Definition of Done ensures consistent completion standards.
- Defect prevention is more effective than defect correction.
- Continuous quality measurement enables informed decision-making.
- Strong quality governance improves customer satisfaction and delivery confidence.

---

## Governance Principles

- Build quality into every stage of delivery.
- Verify work against agreed quality standards.
- Use measurable quality metrics to monitor performance.
- Continuously improve quality practices through feedback and learning.
- Ensure every deliverable satisfies the agreed Definition of Done.
