
# Agile Operating Model

## Overview

This folder defines the Agile Operating Model for the **Delivery Command Center – Enterprise Agile Delivery & Governance Framework**.

It explains how Agile teams, roles, ceremonies, and governance structures work together to ensure consistent delivery, alignment, and transparency across multiple streams of work.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this section is to demonstrate how enterprise Agile delivery is structured and operated in a real-world environment.

It focuses on how teams collaborate, how work flows from backlog to delivery, and how governance is maintained across sprints and releases.

---

## Key Components

### Agile Team Structure
Defines roles such as:
- Agile Delivery Lead
- Technical Product Owner
- Development Team
- QA / Testing Support
- Vendor Teams (where applicable)

---

### Agile Ceremonies
Core ceremonies include:
- Sprint Planning
- Daily Stand-ups
- Backlog Refinement
- Sprint Review
- Sprint Retrospective

---

### Work Management Flow
Work flows through:
- Product Backlog → Sprint Backlog → In Progress → Review → Done

---

### Governance Model
Ensures:
- Sprint goals are clearly defined
- Definition of Done is followed
- Risks and dependencies are tracked
- Delivery progress is transparent

---

## Expected Outcome

A well-structured Agile operating model that enables:

- Predictable sprint execution
- Clear role accountability
- Effective team collaboration
- Strong delivery governance
- Continuous improvement across teams

---

## Key Insight

> A strong Agile Operating Model ensures that teams do not just follow Agile ceremonies, but consistently deliver business value through structured collaboration and governance.
